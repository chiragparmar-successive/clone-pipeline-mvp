About Tabi Pass

Why not try to find your "Tabi (travel) Feeling"?

"Tabi Feeling" refers to the "moment when someone's heart is moved during a trip."
It could be an unforgettable dish you encounter in a new place.
You may find yourself lost for words as you listen to the sound of the waves, take a deep breath of the clear mountain air, and gaze upon the scenery that unfolds before your eyes.
What Tabi Pass values ​​is not the fact that you have "traveled," but "an experience that leaves you feeling truly alive."
Find your own "Tabi Feeling" and go on a journey that will fill your heart.

Take a step away from your everyday life and have a memorable experience.

Tabi Pass was created to make "a little luxurious travel" more accessible to everyone.
By offering a fixed discount from the lowest price on the official website, partner hotels lower the cost barrier, allowing you to enjoy experiences you might have given up on before or afford an extra night.
Not only can you feel like you're getting a good deal, but it also improves the quality of the trip itself.
That is the value that Tabi Pass aims to achieve.

 

Bring Japan closer and deeper.

Our mission is to make the charm of Japan feel more familiar to people.
We hope that domestic travelers as well as guests from all over the world will be able to enjoy the unique beauty and stay experience of Japan.
It's not just Tokyo and Kyoto.
A quiet village, a mountain retreat, a small seaside town...
There are many fascinating places in Japan that are still unknown.

Enjoying travel supports Japanese accommodations.

Staying with Tabi Pass not only allows you to enjoy your trip at a great value, but also helps support Japan's hotels and tourism industry.
We partner with under-resourced accommodations to provide digital tools at low cost, and in some cases for free.
This will help hotels grow sustainably and help them pass on Japan's unique "omotenashi" hospitality to the future.

Let's work together to protect and spread Japan's beauty and proud hospitality culture.
Along with Tabi Pass.
